REGIONAL REFUGEE AND MIGRANT RESPONSE PLAN 2016

CLICK TO NAVIGATE

Nearly a million men, women and children have made the hazardous journey to Europe in 2015.

These people have made the impossible choice of attempting these sea crossings in the pursuit of a better life for their families. Most have been forced to flee their homes to escape war and persecution.

CLICK TO NAVIGATE

More than 800,000 of these people have crossed the Aegean from Turkey to Greece. The vast majority, or 92%, of these arrivals to Greece come from the world’s top ten refugee-producing countries, mainly Syria, Afghanistan and Iraq.


CLICK TO NAVIGATE

A humanitarian disaster has been averted thanks to the combined efforts of the countries of transit and arrival alongside UN agencies and humanitarian partners. This joint response provided life-saving aid, vital services, reception and of course, access to asylum and relocation within Europe.

It is clear this humanitarian emergency will continue, at least into 2016, requiring a coordinated and consistent response throughout Europe with unified government policies and complementary humanitarian interventions

For 2016, the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees and the International Organization for Migration are convening a Regional Refugee and Migration Response Plan.


Bringing together 65 humanitarian partners in 6 countries, the RRMRP represents a comprehensive strategy and appeal to structure the interventions of the aid community, in support of national governments’ own capacity.


CLICK TO NAVIGATE

The Strategic Goals of the RRMP are to:

  1. 1. Build and complement Government capacity to respond. (active hyperlink).
  2. 2. Protect and assist refugee and migrant women, girls, boys and men.
  3. 3. Provide access to asylum and solutions.
  4. 4. Ensure a coordinated and efficient response.


CLICK TO NAVIGATE

Strategic Goal One: To design and implement a response that supports and complements Governments’ existing capacities to manage migration and ensure effective access to asylum and solutions for those in need of international protection, while at the same time investing in and building this capacity.


Government personnel have been on the front line helping refugees and migrants. The RRMRP will ensure that additional capacity building and support is available to further these efforts.


CLICK TO NAVIGATE

Strategic Goal Two: To ensure that refugee and migrant women, girls, boys and men access protection and assistance in a participatory manner, with particular attention to specific needs. Protection-centred assistance should be delivered in a manner that respects age, gender and diversity and is appropriate to the specific characteristics of the movement.


Vulnerable refugees and migrants are in urgent need of protection and assistance, and are increasingly at risk of exploitation.


CLICK TO NAVIGATE

Strategic Goal Three: To strengthen national capacities and protection systems and ensure access to longer-term solutions for refugees and migrants who may become stranded or may want to avail themselves of the protection of these countries.


With some refugees and migrants becoming stranded on route, alternative solutions, including access to asylum, humanitarian admission, family reunification, and voluntary return, are needed.


CLICK TO NAVIGATE

Strategic Goal Four:To strengthen partnership and coordination within the humanitarian community and with governments, both in setting common goals and in establishing national-level coordination structures and information analysis, that ensure an efficient and coordinated response.


The humanitarian spirit shown by volunteers and organizations has been outstanding in 2015. In 2016, the RRMRP aims to move towards a more structured response.
